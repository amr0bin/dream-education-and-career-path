The principle underneath all of this
Readiness is not a credential. It is the combination of knowledge, relationships, evidence, and positioning that allows me to act with authority in the next context. Each phase of this checklist asks the same question: not "have I completed the requirement?" but "am I genuinely prepared to contribute?" Those are different questions, and only the second one matters.

Two-year review
Conduct a formal two-year review of the career direction and primary contribution
The question that should be asked at every major decision point — not resolved once, but held honestly throughout

Is this career path designed to maximise impact on the depression treatment gap, or is it designed to maximise personal meaning and fulfilment while also being genuinely useful? These are not the same question. The answer will change as the career develops, and that is acceptable — but the question needs to be asked explicitly and honestly, not avoided. The discipline is not to resolve it once but to hold it across the twenty years of this work.

  • Set a recurring calendar commitment for this review — every two years, at minimum
  • Ask: what is the primary contribution I am making? Is that the contribution I most want to make? Is it the contribution the problem most needs?
  • Ask: what am I not doing that the path called for? Is that a problem or an evolution?
  • Ask: where is the freedom to act, and am I using it?

This is the item that makes all the other items worth completing. A career that cannot ask this question is a career that has stopped paying attention.
