# Three Assets, Ten Years

*A trajectory map for the three public faces of the work — what each becomes, and how it gets there, in service of the whole-portfolio mission: scalable, accessible care for depression.*

The three assets are not three businesses. They are three organs of one body. **Therapeutic Studio** produces the thing worth scaling. **Accessible Learning Labs** is the discipline that scales it without letting it degrade. **andrearobin.com** is the trust layer that makes both legible to the people who can fund, partner, hire, and amplify them. Read each one down its own column, then read across the phases to see how they move together.

The phases below track the credential and career arc rather than calendar years, so the map holds even if the timeline flexes:

- **Foundation** — now through the early health-sector role and first short courses
- **Formation** — the graduate and clinical-training years (LSHTM MSc, MA, first publications, provisional registration)
- **Integration** — the doctoral years and licensure, when the streams converge
- **Deployment** — full authority; the Tuesday on the far side

---

## 1. Therapeutic Studio

*Houses Make Space and Other Bodies.*

**What it is today:** a living, open studio — creative-health practice shared in public, honestly framed as a record of questions in a young field where the evidence is still being gathered.

**What it becomes:** an evidence-based, manualized, transferable depression intervention with its own training system and a published evidence base — intervention IP that can travel beyond the room it was born in.

**How it gets there:**

*Foundation.* The single highest-leverage move is the smallest: add a validated depression-screening measure at the start and end of each Make Space cycle. The day you have even simple pre/post data, Make Space stops being a beautiful practice and becomes a program with a claim you can defend — and that one dataset becomes the spine of the tools paper, the systematic review's real-world anchor, the grant application, and the social-prescribing pitch. In parallel, begin a structured reflective journal of every session and a light participant-feedback loop. The Substack continues as the public record.

*Formation.* Therapeutic Studio becomes your empirical *site* — the place your research draws its data from. Other Bodies graduates from a 52-card deck into a documented, citable set of clinical instruments (the emotional compass, the topography, the emotion stories), described well enough that someone else could use them faithfully. The systematic review and the first qualitative study of participant experience are published. The Substack matures from reflective journal into a public lab notebook the field begins to cite. Once provisional registration allows, expressive approaches integrate into a real clinical caseload, so the practice is now generating both research data and clinical knowledge.

*Integration.* This is where the mission lives or dies. Make Space is manualized into a facilitator-training curriculum, then a train-the-trainer architecture, then culturally adapted and evaluated in low-resource settings through the LSHTM partnerships — with local leads holding first authorship. Other Bodies is open-licensed so it can travel freely. A feasibility trial and the first implementation studies are published.

*Deployment.* Therapeutic Studio is intervention IP + an evidence base + a self-propagating training system. It very likely outgrows being "your studio" and becomes a small program or nonprofit run partly by facilitator-trainers who don't need you in the room. That independence is the proof the multiplier multiplied.

**Its role in the mission:** Therapeutic Studio answers the question *what travels.* It is the source of the thing that is worth scaling at all.

## 2. Accessible Learning Labs

*Houses Fieldwork.*

**What it is today:** accessibility and UDL consulting for organizations, with Fieldwork as the public-scholarship arm — the practice where your fifteen years of learning-systems expertise meets disability justice.

**What it becomes:** the methodology-and-dissemination engine of the whole portfolio — the place where your rarest skill, designing fidelity-preserving training systems for accessible care, is named, packaged, published, and turned into field-shaping influence. It is also the income that funds the credential years.

**How it gets there:**

*Foundation.* ALL does the load-bearing practical work: it funds the reorientation and builds case studies, while Fieldwork compounds audience and credibility. The deliberate move is to steer the consulting toward *health and mental-health-practitioner training* rather than generic course design, so the work you are paid to produce is already the raw material for implementation-science papers later.

*Formation.* ALL becomes the place where your actual superpower gets named and methodized. The consulting case studies start to read as applied implementation science. Fieldwork narrows from broad accessibility toward the specific, underserved intersection that is yours: disability, depression, and access. The conceptual paper — that designing the training system which delivers an intervention is as important as the intervention itself — is the intellectual seed ALL exists to grow.

*Integration.* ALL is now the bridge that carries learning-systems design into global mental health. Its methodology — how you take something that worked in one room and make it deliverable, faithfully, by a non-specialist, at scale, accessibly — becomes a documented, publishable method, applied directly to the Make Space training system. Fieldwork matures into keynotes; the inquiry begins to take book shape.

*Deployment.* ALL is the dissemination engine and the public methodology platform: publications, a premium consulting offer rooted in genuine rarity, keynotes at global mental health and arts-in-health gatherings, and the long-form writing (the book) that moves your contribution from the field's periphery toward its centre.

**Its role in the mission:** Accessible Learning Labs answers the question *how it travels, and who can be trained to carry it.* It is the discipline that keeps scaling from becoming dilution.

## 3. andrearobin.com

*Currently the professional home where organizations and hiring managers find you and see, through your work, that you are a credible, trusted advisor.*

**What it is today:** a learning-design portfolio that demonstrates credibility through anonymized case studies and frameworks — built to convert a hiring manager's attention into trust.

**What it becomes:** the authoritative personal hub of a clinician-researcher-practitioner — the canonical front door and trust layer that routes every kind of opportunity (organizations, collaborators, funders, students, journalists, conference organizers) to the right part of the work, and carries the authority to make that routing land.

**How it gets there:**

*Foundation.* It stays the professional anchor and sharpens its positioning toward health and mental-health systems specifically. The "trusted advisor" frame deepens: this is where a health authority or research institution confirms you are credible before they hire or partner. Nothing about its core job changes yet — it just gets more pointed about the sector.

*Formation.* As research literacy, the global-mental-health credential, and the first publications arrive, the site's identity shifts from "learning design specialist" to "learning-design specialist building genuine standing at the intersection of knowledge translation and mental health." It becomes the **bridge identity** — the place that shows you are not a learning designer making a hopeful pivot, but someone deliberately constructing a rare profile, with publications and credentials to prove the trajectory is real. Case studies begin to include mental-health-adjacent work.

*Integration.* Post-MA and into the doctorate and licensure, andrearobin.com becomes the unifying hub. It carries the authority markers — the doctorate, Registered Psychologist standing, the publication list, speaking history — and it links out cleanly to the clinical practice, Therapeutic Studio, Accessible Learning Labs, and the writing. Its canonical "about" is the story of the whole combination, and it begins doing conversion work that has nothing to do with employment: it turns your body of work into grant invitations, research partnerships, student inquiries, referrals, and speaking requests.

*Deployment.* andrearobin.com is the trust-and-routing layer for a field-shaping figure. Its job has scaled from *"hire me for learning design"* to *"this is who I am across all of it, here is the proof, and here is the door to each part."* A health system finds the consulting; a funder finds the research; a journalist finds the public writing; a prospective student finds the teaching; a person seeking care finds the practice. One front door, many credible destinations.

**Its role in the mission:** andrearobin.com is the *credibility and routing layer.* Therapeutic Studio and Accessible Learning Labs do the work; andrearobin.com is how the right people come to trust it and find their way in.

## How the three move together

Picture them as a single system across the decade:

| | Therapeutic Studio | Accessible Learning Labs | andrearobin.com |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Function** | What travels | How it travels | Why people trust it |
| **Foundation** | Add outcome measurement; reframe as a program | Fund the years; steer toward health | Sharpen toward health systems |
| **Formation** | Empirical site; tools published | Methodology named; Fieldwork narrows | Bridge identity; publications appear |
| **Integration** | Manualized, adapted, open-licensed | Method published; applied to the curriculum | Unifying hub; authority markers |
| **Deployment** | Self-propagating intervention IP | Dissemination engine; the book | Field-shaping front door |

The moment to watch for — the one that proves the whole thesis — is when the **Make Space facilitator-training curriculum (Therapeutic Studio's content) is built using Accessible Learning Labs' learning-systems methodology, evaluated as a task-shifting model, and presented to the world through andrearobin.com's credibility.** That single artifact — a creative-health intervention, made transferable by a rigorous training system, designed accessibly from the start, tested in a low-resource setting, carried by a trusted public identity — *is* the thesis of the entire career. None of the three can produce it alone.

## Two design choices to make on purpose

**Keep the two Substacks distinct early, converge late.** Fieldwork (practitioners and systems) and the Therapeutic Studio journal (creative health and participants) serve different readers. Let them grow separately and cross-pollinate, then unify toward a single public voice when the keynotes and book arrive — rather than merging prematurely and blurring both.

**Open the intervention; monetize the methodology.** Keeping Other Bodies and the practice sessions free is mission-aligned and builds reach. The *training system* is the thing that can carry premium value and fund the rest. Give away what should travel; charge for the rare skill that makes it travel well.

## What to hold

Therapeutic Studio is the *what.* Accessible Learning Labs is the *how.* andrearobin.com is the *why-you-can-be-trusted.* You are not running three projects competing for your attention — you are building one engine with three visible faces, and every hour spent on any of them, if pointed correctly, compounds the other two.
